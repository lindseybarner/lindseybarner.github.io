Erion Barner, Ph.D. subtitle: Seattle, WA cover-img: "img/op_50_esophagus_6434A-a_0030_t0-5_z_195-rot65_roi_sharp.png" --- I am a microscope builder and hardware developer with expertise in image processing and deep learning. I am seeking to work on cutting-edge research that will advance human health and patient care.

For my Ph.D. thesis, I developed the first multi-resolution open-top light-sheet microscope [1], enabled by novel optics (patent pending) [2,3]. We have demonstrated the potential for this device to improve breast cancer staging accuracy and patient management after surgery [4]. The final chapter of my thesis focused on developing artificial intelligence approaches to assist pathologists with diagnosing esophageal cancer and dysplasia using 3D pathology [5]. My latest conference poster may be viewed here.

More recently, my latest research at Nanostring Technologies, Inc. involved developing a light sheet microscopy system for imaging RNA assays and protein analytes in 3D in situ .
